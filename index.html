Toast

Toast, the yeast smothered masterpiece. It is hands down one of the best inventions of human history after the wheel.
In the grand timeline of human achievement, none shines as radiant as the invention of toast. Born of simple yeast, kissed by flame, microorganism utilization, toast transforms the ordinary into the promised bread; a golden slab of wheat bearing the weight of kings and peasants alike.

Crisped edges offer a satisfying crunch, while the softened middle stores flavors anew: honey, butter, and jams from distant lands, each bite a pleasant sensation upon the taste buds. In an age where every modern invention is precious, toast endures as a testament to timelessness and survival, a daily miracle brought by fire. For toast is the true epitome of bread.



Such what Aristoastle has uttered.

Toasting is not an act, it is a habit.
There is no great genius without some touch of toast.
You will never do anything in this world without toast.
There is only one way to avoid criticism: do toast, say toast, and be toast.
It is during our darkest moments that we must focus to see the toast.
